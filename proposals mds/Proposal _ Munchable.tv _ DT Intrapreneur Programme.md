## Operational Structure: The DT Fellowship

The DT Fellow is a self-managed operator who functions across three operational layers. By integrating
these layers, the Fellow reduces the Client’s internal requirements for project management and strategic
translation.

### 1. Functional Layers of the Role

**Layer 3: Strategic Planning:** The Fellow interprets top-management objectives and converts them into
actionable project plans. This minimizes the Client’s need to provide granular direction or logic for new
initiatives.
**Layer 2: Operational Management:** The Fellow manages the workflow, including task breakdown,
bottleneck identification, and real-time course correction. This self-management layer reduces the
Client's supervisory burden.
**Layer 1: Execution:** The Fellow performs the primary work functions and technical tasks required to
meet the project’s defined KPIs.

### 2. Efficiency Model

The Fellowship model is designed to consolidate the traditional three-tier hierarchy into a single point of
delivery:
**Layer Traditional Client Requirement DT Fellow Responsibility
Layer 3 (Strategy)** Client must design the plan. **Fellow** designs the plan.
**Layer 2 (Management)** Client must supervise and troubleshoo **Fellow** self-manages and corrects.
**Layer 1 (Work)** Junior staff executes tasks. **Fellow** executes tasks.

### 3. Service Impact

```
● Minimized Management (Layer 2): Because the Fellow identifies and resolves bottlenecks
internally, the requirement for Client-led project management is significantly reduced.
● Systems Integration (Layer 3): As systems thinkers, Fellows align their daily output with the
Client's long-term strategy, ensuring that operational work supports high-level business goals
without constant realignment meetings.
```

## The L&D Framework: Enabling Full-Stack Performance

The ability of a DT Fellow to operate across Layers 1, 2, and 3 is a direct result of the **DT Learning &
Development (L&D) Program**. This training replaces traditional "on-the-job" learning with a structured
methodology designed to produce self-managed experts.

### 1. Skill Acquisition by Layer

The L&D program focuses on three core competencies that align with the service model:
**First Principles Thinking (Layer 3):** Fellows are trained to decompose high-level business goals into their
fundamental components. This allows them to build implementation plans and customized templates
rather than sticking to existing templates.
**Systems Thinking (Layer 2):** The curriculum covers project auditing, bottleneck identification, and
workflow optimization. This enables the Fellow to recognize and fix inefficiencies before they require
Client intervention. This helps them think holistically and avoid siloed thinking.
**Technical Proficiency (Layer 1):** Fellows undergo rigorous training in the specific tools and platforms
(Google Sheets, Appscript) required to execute the daily work.

### 2. The "Home-Grown Expert" Pipeline

By the time a Fellow is deployed, the L&D program has institutionalized the following behaviors:
**Self-Correction:** The training emphasizes "active feedback loops," where the Fellow reviews their own
output against KPIs, minimizing the need for Client-led Layer 2 supervision.
**Strategic Alignment:** Fellows are taught to view every task through the lens of the Client’s ROI, ensuring
that Layer 1 work consistently serves Layer 3 objectives.

### 3. Impact on Client Resources

The DT L&D process is designed to shift the training and management burden from the Client to the
Fellowship.

Training Burden** Borne by the Client. Pre-managed by DT L&D.
**Strategic Logic** Relies on Client instructions. Derived from First Principles.
